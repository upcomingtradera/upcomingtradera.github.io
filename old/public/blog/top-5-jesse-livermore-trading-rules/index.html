Five Jesse Livermore Trading Rules

Imagine that you are the trader, you’re at a high-stakes poker game, and the legendary Jesse Livermore walks in, ready to dazzle the crowd with his trading rules as if they were his winning hand. The room goes silent, and everyone leans in, eager to hear the secrets that turned this “Boy Plunger” into a Wall Street legend.

  1. Follow the trend: Livermore emphasized the importance of trading in the direction of the market trend. He believed that traders should not fight the trend but instead capitalize on it, as it is more likely to result in profitable trades.
  2. Cut losses quickly: One of Livermore’s key rules was to exit a losing trade as soon as it moved against him. He considered this a crucial aspect of risk management, as it helped to minimize losses and protect trading capital.
  3. Let profits run: Livermore advocated for allowing winning trades to continue as long as the market trend was in favor. He believed that traders should not exit a profitable position prematurely, as it could limit potential gains.
  4. Patience and timing: Livermore stressed the importance of waiting for the right opportunity before entering a trade. He believed that patience was essential in identifying optimal entry points, as entering a trade too early or too late could result in losses.
  5. Manage risk and emotions: Livermore considered effective risk management and emotional control to be critical aspects of successful trading. He advised using stop-loss orders and position sizing to limit losses, as well as maintaining discipline and objectivity to avoid being swayed by fear or greed.

While these rules provide valuable insights, it is important to remember that Livermore’s aggressive trading strategies and lack of consistent discipline also led to multiple bankruptcies throughout his career. As such, these rules should be applied with caution and adapted to individual risk tolerance, trading style, and market conditions.
